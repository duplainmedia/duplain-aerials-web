01 · The Deliverable

What an HOA actually receives.

No abstract reports. Specific files your board can attach to a meeting packet, your management company can file with the county or insurance, and your pond contractor can use to plan next year's treatment.

01

Permit-overlay PDF

A complete visual presentation: every pond captured against the original county permit. Per-pond comparison views, summary findings, and an at-a-glance current-state composite. Built to drop into a board meeting packet.

02

Imagery archive

Full-resolution drone imagery for every pond on the property, indexed and organized by pond ID. Yours to keep, share with the county or insurance, or hand to a successor vendor without losing continuity.

03

Year-over-year baseline

The first year is your reference. Every year after, we shoot the same altitudes, the same angles, and the same time windows, so your board can see what changed and what held. Compliance stops being a guess.

04

Composite current-state map

An on-demand single-page composite, all ponds in current condition, modeled on the original permit-map view. Useful for resident communications and county follow-ups when the board needs one image, not twenty.

02 · The Engagement

How a project actually runs.

The shape of every HOA engagement, from first inbound to year-over-year continuity. Five phases, roughly two months end-to-end on a typical seventeen-pond community.

  1. Phase 01

    Discovery and scope

    A first conversation with your board contact and pond manager about what you actually need. Most HOAs come in asking for "drone photos" and leave with a deliverable scoped to their compliance situation: imaging only, imaging plus overlay analysis, or a full annual program. Scoping is a phone call, not a quote engine.

  2. Phase 02

    Stakeholder introductions

    Before any flight, we meet your lakes-and-waterways committee chair and your pond management company on a site walk. The board introduces the manager, the manager introduces the contractor, and each handoff is a credibility check we expect and respect. Your existing pond vendor stays in the loop the whole way through.

  3. Phase 03

    Insurance and paperwork

    Before we book the flight, your management company receives a signed W-9, a Certificate of Insurance for drone liability, and confirmation of FAA Part 107 licensing. Your HOA and management firm are listed as additional insured. This is the gate that lets payment process; we handle it up front, not after delivery.

  4. Phase 04

    Field capture and processing

    Half a day on site, mid-morning window, every pond flown at consistent altitude. Larger ponds get composite close-ups for plant identification. Then two to three weeks of overlay and analysis work in QGIS, with a sample shared mid-process so your pond manager can sign off on the visual approach. We catch our own errors before you see them.

  5. Phase 05

    Delivery and follow-through

    Final PDF and imagery archive delivered through your management company's preferred channel, with a built-in follow-up window for board questions, label clarifications, and any composite views the chair wants pulled out separately. Next year's quote arrives before you have to ask for it, because most boards want it on the books inside a week of seeing the current year.

04 · How We Fly

Practices that show up in the imagery.

A few decisions that separate water imaging worth paying for from water imaging that ages badly. Most are not visible in the deliverable; they are why the deliverable holds up.

  • 01

    Mid-morning capture, not noon

    The 8 to 10 AM window minimizes glare on the water surface. Calmer winds reduce ripple, dew has burned off, and thermal turbulence has not yet built. Imagery captured here is consistently legible. Imagery captured at noon is consistently glare-blown.

  • 02

    Composite close-ups on larger ponds

    For ponds where a single capture cannot resolve plant identification, we fly seven to eight overlapping close-up segments and composite them in post. The result reads at the same effective resolution as a small-pond capture, regardless of size.

  • 03

    Pre-flight site walk with your pond manager

    Before any flight, we walk the property with whoever is contracting your aquatic treatment. They explain what is permitted, what is treated, and what is currently in dispute. The imagery is targeted to those questions, not generic.

  • 04

    Standard community-notification protocol

    Residents are notified in advance through whatever channel your board normally uses (email blast, posted notice, neighborhood app). We schedule capture around it. No surprises in the air, no surprises in the inbox.

06 · Common Questions

What other boards have asked.

These are the actual questions that have come back from HOAs we have engaged with, or that have been forwarded by board members evaluating the work for their own communities. Each one matters; each one has a clean answer.

Will you do the full compliance interpretation, or just deliver imagery? +

Both, depending on the engagement. Standard scope delivers the imagery and the permit overlay; the interpretation of compliance status is a conversation between your board, your pond manager, and the county. For boards that want a more analytical layer, we offer GIS overlay work that quantifies littoral coverage as a percentage of pond area and identifies year-over-year drift. We do not represent your community to the county. That role stays with your management company.

Do you host the deliverables, or do we keep them? +

Yours. Every PDF and every imagery archive is delivered to your management company directly, in a format your team can store on whatever document system you already use. We retain a copy for our own records and to support next year's flight, but your community is not locked into us. If you ever change vendors, your archive walks with you.

What do we need to communicate to residents about the drone activity? +

Standard practice is a short notice circulated through whatever channel your board normally uses, sent two to three days before the scheduled flight window. We can supply a draft notice if your manager wants one. Residents are not photographed; the focus is on water and shoreline. Any incidental frames that capture a private home at recognizable resolution are cropped or blurred before delivery.

When is the best time of year and time of day to capture? +

Late summer through fall, after the wet season has loaded the system but before the winter dry-down compresses the data. Day-of, mid-morning is the sweet spot. 8 to 10 AM is when wind, glare, and thermal turbulence are all in the workable range. We aim for a calm, dry-following morning and reschedule when weather does not cooperate.

What is your experience with ponds and waterways specifically? +

Active engagements with Florida HOAs in Sarasota County (Deer Creek Association, 17 ponds) and neighboring communities currently in conversation. We work in coordination with regional pond management firms and have completed photogrammetry-based work for the TREE Foundation's chapel hammock biome project, in collaboration with USF GIS. Pond-specific work is the studio's lead service, not a sideline.

Are you Part 107 licensed and insured? +

Yes to both. FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot certified, current. $1M commercial liability through Skywatch. We issue a Certificate of Insurance to your management company before any flight, listing your HOA and the management firm as additional insured. Your management company can file the COI with payable accounts the same week.

What does pricing look like, and what changes year over year? +

Pricing is per pond count, all-in, and the per-pond rate steps down once a community is on a recurring schedule. The first year carries the discovery and overlay-build cost; subsequent years use the same baseline maps and so price lower. The current per-pond schedule is published below, and on our home page. We are happy to put a written quote in front of your board before any commitment.

Will the deliverable be self-explanatory, or will we need a vendor to interpret it? +

Self-explanatory by design. Every pond panel is labeled in plain English, with the original permit map alongside the current capture and a short summary in language a board member with no GIS background can read. We field follow-up questions from chairs and committee members for free in the weeks after delivery; that is part of the engagement, not a separate scope.
